The Problem

Philadelphia's tax system was designed for a W-2 economy, not today's independent workforce. Starting in April 2026, solo business owners face a massive tax increase they weren't expecting.

Real Impact: $50,000 Annual Earner

April 2026 tax bill increases by $1,208 — equivalent to three months' rent

Ongoing annual increase: $3,236

"We are taxing working families out of their homes."

The Solution: The LIFT Act

Create a third tax class for individuals and single-member LLCs, exempting them from the BIRT Net Income Tax while maintaining other tax obligations.

What the LIFT Act Does

  • Exempts sole proprietors and single-member LLCs from BIRT Net Income Tax (5.71%)
  • Maintains Gross Receipts Tax (0.141%)
  • Maintains Net Profits Tax (3.74%)
  • Reduces double-taxation on individual business owners
  • Aligns Philadelphia with state and federal treatment

Bill introduced: November 20, 2025 by Councilman Mike Driscoll
Finance Committee hearing: Expected early February 2026
Effective date: July 1, 2026
